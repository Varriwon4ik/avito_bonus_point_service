# Sprint 1 Retrospective

This retrospective is public and sanitized. It contains no sensitive personal
information or private conflict details.

## What went well

- We delivered all four planned MVP v1 `Must Have` stories (US-05, US-11, US-12,
  US-13) plus a `Should Have` observability story (US-10), and the customer
  accepted the demonstrated increment.
- The issue-linked PR workflow with an extended PR template and
  acceptance-criteria verification kept reviews focused and traceable.
- QA involvement was strong: concurrency tests (US-11) and HTTP-status coverage
  (US-13) gave us confidence in the increment.

## What did not go well

- US-05 could not be demonstrated live because its owner could not connect, and
  ownership had to be reassigned mid-Sprint after technical issues.
- Backlog hygiene lagged at the start: Story Points and MVP-version tracking were
  formalized only partway through the assignment.
- Recording started late, so the first part of the Sprint Review was not captured
  on video.

## Action points

- Pair on or co-own critical PBIs so no single absence blocks a demo, and
  pre-record a backup demo for each MVP item.
- Establish backlog-refinement and estimation as a standing event at the start of
  the Sprint, with MVP-version and Story Points set before work begins.
